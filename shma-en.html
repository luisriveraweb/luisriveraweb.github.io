Sh'ma

Sh`ma: Door

An installation by Oskar Stocker and Luis Rivera

About the project:

Twelve doors of Jewish houses. Set in door frames with no mezuzah* attached. Vulnerable out in the open.

The statement: a door that does not move is a useless object.

If it remains closed forever, it locks out or locks in. If it remains open forever, it means loss and gain. If this door remains half-open in the middle – undecidedly – the hesitant do not enter.

Will thus not experience what lies beyond the threshold. What those who live there call life. The way they live, drink, eat, paint pictures, bow their heads, dance, raise their hands. Are quiet, are loud, argue. Reconcile, in their own language, with their own words. How their children play, their sick suffer and how they see hopes crumble or come true.

The essence of the door is to be moved.

Karin Peschka
Oskar Stocker l Luis Rivera, Sh`ma, Synagoge, Graz 2019
